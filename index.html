Career Wisdom

Mentor Tips

Real advice from professionals who've been through it.

📄

Tailor Every Resume

Customize your resume for each role you apply to. Mirror the job description's language and highlight only what's relevant.

🤝

Network Before You Need It

Build relationships before you're job hunting. Attend events, reach out on LinkedIn, and offer value before asking for favors.

💬

Master the Follow-Up

Send a thoughtful thank-you note within 24 hours of every interview. It sets you apart and keeps you top of mind.

🎯

Quality Over Quantity

Apply to fewer jobs with stronger, more targeted applications rather than blasting your resume to hundreds of postings.

📈

Track Your Impact

Quantify everything on your resume. Numbers and outcomes speak louder than duties. Replace "managed" with "grew X by Y%."

🧘

Protect Your Energy

The job search is a marathon. Build in recovery time, celebrate small wins, and don't tie your self-worth to response rates.

We provide curated resources, career advice, and tools like MentorBot to guide you every step of the way — from your first resume to your first offer letter.

Step 01 — Design

Started in Figma

Before writing a single line of code, I mapped out the full user experience in Figma. I created low-fidelity wireframes to define the layout and content structure, then built high-fidelity mockups to nail the visual identity — color palette, typography, spacing, and component design. Figma let me iterate quickly on the look and feel before committing to code, which saved significant time and kept the design intentional rather than accidental.

WireframingUI DesignColor SystemComponent Design
🤖
Step 02 — AI Collaboration

Using Claude as a Development Partner

I used Claude (an LLM by Anthropic) as an active collaborator throughout the build — not just to generate boilerplate, but to think through architecture decisions, debug layout issues, and iterate on the design system. I would describe what I wanted in plain English, review what the AI produced, then refine my prompts based on the output. This taught me how to communicate clearly with AI, understand its limitations, and use it as a force multiplier for my own ideas rather than a replacement for thinking.

"I didn't just ask AI to write code — I used it to think out loud, explore options, and move faster while staying in control of every decision."

⚡
Step 03 — Build

Translating Design to Code

With the Figma designs as my blueprint, I built the site in HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript. I kept all styles self-contained in the HTML file for simplicity and portability. Key features included a fixed glass-morphism navbar, scroll-triggered animations, an interactive tip carousel, and a fully responsive layout that works on mobile and desktop. Every component was built to match the Figma mockup as closely as possible, reinforcing the discipline of design-to-code fidelity.

HTMLCSS VariablesVanilla JSResponsive DesignAnimations
🚀
Step 04 — Deploy & Refine

Shipped on GitHub Pages, Refined with AI

The site is hosted on GitHub Pages with continuous deployment — every commit to main automatically updates the live site. After the initial launch I continued using AI to refine the design, improve accessibility, and add new sections like this one. The result is a workflow I can apply to any future project: design in Figma → build with AI assistance → deploy on GitHub → iterate continuously. This cycle makes me significantly faster and more effective as a developer.
